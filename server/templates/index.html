System Flow

How It Works

From river sensor to emergency alert in seconds — a fully automated pipeline.

1
Sense

ESP8266 + HC-SR04 measures water distance every 10 seconds

2
Transmit

Authenticated HTTPS POST with API-key header to the Flask server

3
Process

Server validates, stores reading, evaluates thresholds in real time

4
Alert

Dashboard updates live; SMS + email fire to emergency contacts

Alert System

Three-Level Warning System

Graduated alerts give communities time to prepare and evacuate before conditions become critical.

SAFE
Alert Level 1
Normal Water Level

Sensor distance > 200 cm. Water levels are within normal range. Green LED is active. System continues routine monitoring every 10 seconds.

  • Green LED illuminated
  • No alerts triggered
  • Continuous data logging
WARNING
Alert Level 2
Rising Water Level

Sensor distance 50–100 cm. Water levels are rising above safe threshold. Yellow LED, warning beep, and automatic SMS + email to responders.

  • Yellow LED + warning tone
  • SMS & email alert sent
  • Dashboard alert created
DANGER
Alert Level 3
Critical Flood Danger

Sensor distance < 50 cm. Immediate evacuation required. Red LED, rapid alarm beeps (×3), and urgent alerts to all channels.

  • Red LED + rapid alarm
  • Urgent SMS & email blast
  • Immediate action required

Context

Why South Sudan Needs This System

South Sudan experiences severe annual flooding driven by heavy rainfall in the Ethiopian Highlands and the Equatorial Lakes region, affecting millions of people across the Nile basin.

With limited infrastructure and communication networks, communities are often caught unprepared. A low-cost, IoT-based early warning system can provide critical lead time for evacuation and emergency response.

Early warning saves lives — even 30 minutes' notice can enable evacuation
