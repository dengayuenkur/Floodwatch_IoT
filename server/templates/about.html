Project Overview

About the Project

An academic and humanitarian research initiative at the intersection of IoT engineering, cybersecurity, and disaster risk management for South Sudan.

Mission

Protecting Communities Through Technology

Flooding is one of the most devastating natural hazards affecting South Sudan, displacing hundreds of thousands of people each year and destroying livelihoods, infrastructure, and agricultural land.

This project designs, implements, and evaluates a low-cost, secure, IoT-based flood early warning system that can be deployed in low-resource settings — using commodity hardware, open-source software, and cellular internet connectivity.

The core research questions are: How can IoT technology provide effective and secure real-time flood warnings in resource-constrained environments? What cybersecurity controls are necessary to protect an IoT water monitoring system from attack?
